Characteristics of Dissociative disorders

Characteristics of dissociative disorders include;

  • Feeling disconnected from themselves
  • Experiencing problems with handling intense emotions
  • Having sudden mood shifts, e.g. an unexpected feeling of sadness out of nowhere
  • Feeling that the world is not real or distorted
  • Experiencing depression or anxiety problems, sometimes both
  • Sustaining memory problems unrelated to physical injury
  • Having significant memory lapses, e.g. forgetting personal information
  • The individual feeling like they have to behave in a certain way
  • The individual experiencing identity confusion, e.g. behaving in a way that they would normally feel uncomfortable
  • The individual experiencing other cognitive issues, e.g. concentration problems.
